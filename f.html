SCIENCE LAB

The chemical blueprint of human performance and muscle hypertrophy.

T

Testosterone

The primary male sex hormone. It is the main driver of protein synthesis within muscle fibers, leading to increases in muscle mass and strength. It also plays a vital role in fat distribution and bone density.

GH

Growth Hormone

Secreted by the pituitary gland, GH acts as a cell regenerator. It promotes fat burning for energy and stimulates the liver to produce IGF-1, which aids in the recovery of joints and strained muscle tissue.

I

Insulin

The most powerful anabolic hormone. Its function is to open "doors" to muscle cells to allow glucose and amino acids to enter. This immediately stops muscle breakdown after training and initiates the building process.

IGF

IGF-1

Insulin-like Growth Factor-1. This is the actual mediator of Growth Hormone's action. It contributes directly to muscle hypertrophy and cellular division, effectively increasing the number of muscle fibers.

C

Cortisol

The catabolic stress hormone. It is secreted during extreme stress or lack of sleep. Cortisol breaks down muscle protein to convert it into energy. It must be managed through rest and nutrition to prevent muscle loss.

T3

Thyroid (T3)

The engine of metabolism. T3 controls the speed of calorie burning and the efficiency of oxygen use. A balance in this hormone ensures fat loss while maintaining body temperature and high energy levels.

E

Epinephrine

Also known as Adrenaline, it is secreted during high-intensity training. It increases blood flow to muscles, dilates airways, and stimulates the breakdown of fat to provide immediate explosive energy.

M

Myostatin

The genetic brake. A protein produced naturally by the body to set a limit on muscle size. In bodybuilding, the goal is to reduce its activity chemically or genetically to allow muscle growth beyond natural limits.
